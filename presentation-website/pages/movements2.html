Romanian New Wave

A few important Romanian New Wave directors and their films;

  • Cristian Mungiu and his film 4 luni, 3 saptamâni si 2 zile

    Cristian Mungiu is a Romanian filmmaker born in Iaşi, Romania in 1968. His debut film, Occident, was premiered in Directors’ Fortnight in Cannes in 2002 and was an audience hit in Romania. In 2007, his second feature, 4 Months, 3 Weeks and 2 Days, was awarded the Palme d’Or in Cannes. The film later received several awards as best film of the year from various international associations of film critics. It also won the European Film Academy awards for best film and best director.

  • Corneliu Porumboiu and his film Politist, adjectiv

    Romanian filmmaker Corneliu Porumboiu, winner of the 2006 Cannes Film Festival Camera d’Or has made one of the most original and intelligent comedies of recent years, 12:08 East of Bucharest. Convinced that "historical truth does not exist", he has created an ironic and biting portrait of contemporary Romania through the memories of that December 22 of 1989.

  • Cristi Puiu and his film Moartea domnului Lăzărescu

    Cristi Puiu is a Romanian film director and screenwriter. Puiu's first interest in art was painting and in 1992, he was admitted as a student at the Painting Department of Ecole Superieure d'Arts Visuels in Geneva. After the first year he switched to film studies at the same school and graduated in 1996. After returning to Romania, Cristi Puiu started directing and writing his first films, generally enjoying critical success.
